Sensex slips in red; BHEL, Sterlite, Bharti decline
ET 10 JAN, 2013, 02.23PM IST, ECONOMICTIMES.COM
StockQuote T 232.25 232.2 

MUMBAI: The Sensex pared its intraday gains and slipped in the negative territory on Thursday, led by losses in realty, power, capital goods and metal sectors, on the back of subdued opening of European markets. 
At 02:00 pm, the 30-share index was at 19,612.48, down 54.11 points or 0.28 per cent. The index touched a high of 19,783.75 and a low of 19,612.48 in trade today. 
The Nifty was at 5,953.85, down 17.65 points or 0.30 per cent. It touched a high of 6,005.15 and a low of 5,951 in trade today. 
The BSE Midcap Index was down 0.60 per cent and the BSE Smallcap Index declined 0.58 per cent. 
The BSE Realty Index was down 0.87 per cent, the BSE Power Index was 0.83 per cent lower and the BSE Capital Goods Index declined 0.78 per cent. The BSE Auto Index was up 0.26 per cent, the BSE Oil & Gas Index moved 0.09 per cent higher and the BSE Bankex edged up 0.06 per cent. 
BHELBSE -2.01 % (2.11 per cent), Sterlite IndustriesBSE 1.17 % (1.66 per cent), Bharti AirtelBSE -1.58 % (1.29 per cent), Hindalco IndustriesBSE -2.23 % (1.14 per cent) and HDFC (1.08 per cent) were the top Sensex losers. 
Tata MotorsBSE -0.97 % (2.10 per cent), ONGCBSE -3.28 % (1.37 per cent), HDFC BankBSE -0.89 % (0.79 per cent), Coal IndiaBSE -1.38 % (0.44 per cent) and WiproBSE 6.10 % (0.30 per cent) were among the major Sensex gainers. 
Tata Motors continued to move higher after leading global brokerages CLSA and Credit Suisse upgraded their ratings on the stock, citing multiple product launches from JLR and receding fears of slowdown in China. 
The market breadth was negative on the BSE with 1,115 gainers against 1,733 losers. 
The foreign institutional investors bought shares worth Rs 848.95 crore on Wednesday as per the provisional data from the National Stock Exchange. 
The European markets had a subdued opening. The FTSE 100 was up 0.09 per cent, the CAC 40 slipped 0.37 per cent and the DAX edged 0.04 per cent higher.
