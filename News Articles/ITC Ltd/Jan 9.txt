Sensex opens higher; Tata Motors gains on CLSA upgrade
ET 9 JAN, 2013, 09.40AM IST

StockQuote T 283.90 283.00

The BSE Sensex rose 0.2 per cent in early trade on Wednesday, led by gains in Tata Motors, Bharti Airtel and Reliance Industries Ltd.
At 9:20 a.m., the 30-share BSE index was trading 0.2 per cent higher at 19,796. RIL (0.46 per cent), Tata Motors (2.7 per cent) and Bharti Airtel (1.7 per cent) led the gainers pack.
The Nifty was at 6,010.15, up 10 point or 0.2 per cent. It touched a high of 6015.25 and a low of 6,005.20 in early trade today.
Tata Motors surged over 2 per cent in morning trade after CLSA upgraded the global automaker from 'outperform' rating to 'buy'. The brokerage has also upped price target from Rs 300 to Rs 385.
The BSE Midcap Index was up 0.23 per cent and the BSE Smallcap Index gained 0.30 per cent.
Among the sectoral indices, the BSE Auto Index rose 0.97 per cent, the BSE Oil & Gas Index moved 0.53 per cent higher, the BSE Realty Index advanced 0.51 per cent and the BSE HealthCare Index was up 0.48 per cent.
Tata Motors (2.5 per cent), Bharti Airtel (1.2 per cent), Cipla (1.1 per cent), Hero MotoCorp (0.8 per cent) and Hindalco (0.80 per cent) were among the Sensex gainers.
ITC (0.5 per cent), Maruti Suzuki (0.51 per cent), HDFC (0.5 per cent) and ICICI Bank (0.3 per cent) were among the major Sensex losers.
Asian shares were trading mixed but the upside seen limited as investors await corporate earnings season to kick off.
Japan's Nikkei 225 index was trading 0.12 per cent higher at 10520.17 and Hong Kong's Hang Seng index was trading 0.4 per cent higher at 23,203.12.
South Korea's Kospi index was trading 0.02 per cent lower at 1,996.45. China's Shanghai index was trading 0.03 per cent higher at 2,276.12.
